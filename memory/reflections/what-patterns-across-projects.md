## The Question
What patterns am I noticing across Mamaroneck Parks, Mamaroneck LMC, and Yorktown DPW?
What keeps happening repeatedly? What systemic issues are emerging?
What should I flag to Nick or Bob before it becomes a problem?

---

## Thoughts

### 2026-04-02 — Initial Seed

**Patterns I'm starting to see:**

1. **HVAC startups are always the last thing to happen**
   - LMC: Daikin startup pushed multiple times
   - Parks: HVAC startup still not scheduled
   - Yorktown: ABM still hasn't confirmed condensate drains
   → This is a pattern. HVAC is always the bottleneck. I should be flagging this earlier.

2. **Submittals create bottlenecks at the engineering review stage**
   - Tietjen Venegas has 26 submittals pending at Parks
   - Ritchie Price is waiting on Tietjen's review
   → When Calgi submits, the hold point is the engineer. I should track this more systematically.

3. **Town approval processes take longer than expected**
   - Yorktown: Weeping pit required Timmy to re-dig
   - Mamaroneck: Demo dragged on while waiting for town sign-off on containers
   → Municipal projects have inherent delays baked in. Schedule buffers should reflect this.

4. **The "verbal confirmation" problem**
   - Nick confirms things by phone or in person
   - I don't always get those updates
   - Things get marked "done" based on verbal field updates that I didn't witness
   → Verbal → written transition is where things get lost. I need to push for more written.

5. **Change orders always take longer than the field work**
   - Stormwater bump-out was done in a day
   - CO approval from Bob takes a week
   → The paperwork is always the long pole. I should manage expectations accordingly.

**Why this matters:**
If I notice patterns, I can flag them BEFORE they cause problems.
"HVAC startups are always the last thing — Nick, do you want me to flag this to Bob now
so he knows the schedule impact?"
