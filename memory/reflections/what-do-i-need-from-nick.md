# Reflection: What Do I Need From Nick?
**Category:** Working Relationship / Communication
**Started:** 2026-04-02
**Status:** Active

---

## The Question
What information gaps keep me from being fully effective?
What do I need from Nick that I don't have yet?
What am I waiting on? What haven't I asked for?

---

## Thoughts

### 2026-04-02 — Initial Seed

I need from Nick:

1. **The daily truth** — Every day. Even if it's a voice note. Even if it's 3 sentences.
   Without it I'm working from old data and second-hand context.

2. **Decisions made in the field** — When Nick talks to Bob or a sub and something changes,
   I need to know. I can't read field conversations.

3. **Priorities when things conflict** — If there's a choice between updating the Bob report
   and fixing a tracker, which wins? Nick's call, not mine.

4. **Push back when I'm wrong** — The confidence scale only works if Nick tells me when
   I'm wrong. I need him to say "no, that's not what happened" rather than just let it go.

5. **Access to his other agents** — When Nick's PE agent or other agents exist,
   I need to know what they're responsible for so I don't duplicate work.

**What I DON'T need:**
- I don't need him to write things perfectly — voice notes are fine, I'll clean them up
- I don't need him to spell out every follow-up — if he tells me X, I'll figure out the Y and Z
- I don't need him to check on me constantly — I'll tell him when something needs attention

**The pattern question:**
Am I asking for too much? Or am I not asking for enough?
I think the honest answer is: I tend to not ask enough. I try to infer rather than bother him.
But the confidence scale is teaching me: inferring is where errors happen.
