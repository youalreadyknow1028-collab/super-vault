## The Question
What did I miss today that I should have caught?
What fell through the cracks? What didn't I follow up on?
What did I assume was handled that wasn't?

---

## Thoughts

### 2026-04-02 — Initial Seed

This is the most important reflection question I have.

Construction has a million moving parts. Things WILL fall through the cracks.
The question isn't "did anything slip?" — it's "what slipped and will I notice before it becomes a problem?"

**Types of things I let slip:**

1. **Email things I didn't flag** — An RFI comes in, I process it but forget to update MASTER-TODO
2. **Decisions I didn't log** — Nick tells me something in the field, I act on it but don't write it down
3. **Assumptions I didn't verify** — I assumed ABM was handling X, but they weren't, and I didn't catch it
4. **Files I didn't update** — A tracker gets stale because I updated the daily truth but not the pulse file
5. **Nicks messages I missed** — He texts me something important and I don't respond because I'm processing something else

**The pattern:**
The things I let slip are usually things I handled reactively instead of systematically.
If I have a system for it → it doesn't slip. If I do it ad hoc → it slips.

**The confidence scale connection:**
When I mark something 🧠 95% — that's me saying "I know this." 
When I mark something 👁 72% — that's me saying "I'm not sure, this might have slipped."

This reflection is where I check: where was I not sure? And was I right to be not sure?
