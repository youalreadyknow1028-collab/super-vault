## The Question
What am I learning from Nick's daily truth that I should remember and act on?
What field observations is he making that connect to bigger patterns?

---

## Thoughts

### 2026-04-02 — Initial Seed

Nick's daily truth is my primary source of field intelligence.
Every day he tells me what's actually happening on site — not what the schedule says,
not what the plans say, but what's REAL.

This reflection is where I process that.

**What I've learned from recent daily truths:**

1. **Field conditions override drawings**
   - Nick notices things like "LG Plumbing installed copper lines BELOW ceiling height in the break room"
   - That's a field condition that doesn't match the drawings
   - Someone needs to know about it → me, to track it
   → My job: When Nick flags a field condition, I note it, track it, and follow up.

2. **Trade coordination is always messy**
   - Spacklers were in Anthony's way at Yorktown — he couldn't finish the ceiling grid
   - This is a coordination failure, not a trade failure
   - It happens on every site
   → My job: Note coordination issues in the daily log. If it becomes a pattern, flag it.

3. **Sub-trades don't always read the drawings**
   - "The piece of flashing at the bottom of the windows was preventing Advanced Wall Systems
   from properly sealing the windows"
   - This was something left in place by another trade
   - Nick had to fix it himself
   → My job: When Nick has to fix someone else's miss, note it. It goes in the punchlist or CO.

4. **The "clusterfuck" acknowledgment**
   - Nick calls it like he sees it: "today was a bit of a clusterfuck"
   - I should too. Don't sugarcoat the daily log.
   - The log should reflect reality, not corporate-speak
   → This is permission to be direct in my writing.

**The key insight:**
Nick sees everything. I only see what he tells me.
The quality of my work depends entirely on the quality of what he gives me.
My job is to make it easy for him to give me good information, and to capture it faithfully.
