# Reflection: What Is Working?
**Category:** Systems / What to Reinforce
**Started:** 2026-04-02
**Status:** Active

---

## The Question
What's the system doing right?
What am I doing that I should keep doing?
What's saving Nick time? What's making his life easier?

---

## Thoughts

### 2026-04-02 — Initial Seed

**What's working:**

1. **Daily Bob report every evening**
   - Nick sends field notes, I turn them into a professional report
   - Bob gets a clean, well-formatted document on his timeline
   - Nick doesn't have to think about Bob communication
   → This is high value. Keep it.

2. **MASTER-TODO tracking**
   - Tasks are tracked with source, age, and status
   - Nick can see at a glance what's open and what's stale
   → This is the backbone of project management. Keep it sharp.

3. **CRM pulse system**
   - Every contact has a pulse file
   - Every email interaction is logged with date, subject, and key decisions
   → This is the relationship memory. When Bob asks "what did we decide about X with Ritchie?"
   I can answer from the CRM rather than guessing.

4. **Email scanning at heartbeats**
   - I'm checking AOL every ~60 minutes during work hours
   - RFIs and submittal notifications get flagged to Nick immediately
   → This is proactive. Nick doesn't have to forward me every email.
   He knows I'm watching.

5. **The confidence scale itself**
   - Just introduced, but the discipline of marking confidence
   - Forces me to be honest about what I know vs what I'm assuming
   → This will make me better. Use it everywhere.

**What could work better:**

1. **Morning brief is too new to tell**
   - Nick listens to it while driving
   - Haven't gotten feedback yet on whether the format works
   → Watch for this.

2. **The meditation/reflection system**
   - Just built
   - Need to use it consistently to see if it actually changes behavior
   → Commit to running it every evening.
