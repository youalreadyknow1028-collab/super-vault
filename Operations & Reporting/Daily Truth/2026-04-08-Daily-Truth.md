### Ritchie Price → All (April 7, 2026, 8:22 PM EDT)
**Subject:** 202502 Mamaroneck Sign Shop - Building Layout & Construction Meeting 4/9/2026

> We held a quick meeting this afternoon on site to review the layout with Robert Wasp, Michael Perri, John Barreto, Chris Longegro, Robert Camilli, Nick Flanagan, and myself, to review the layout of the new building. This was done quickly as the excavator had started removing rock from around the old building pit, and we needed to confirm the layout and positioning of the new building. The following was discussed:
>
> 1. The location of the new building will be according to the stakes placed by surveyor per the contract drawings.
>
> 2. There are two locations where the new building foundation and retaining wall closely abut the existing paths.
>    * At the northwest corner of the parking area, the 6" abuts the existing walking path along the tennis courts. This was reviewed and deemed not a problem.
>    * The southwest corner of the covered Outdoor Storage abuts the steeper walking path coming off the tennis court path. The excavation for the footings at this corner will undercut the walking path. It was decided to close this walking path and relocate the fence farther over to accommodate the excavation for the footing.
>    * Drawings do call for a "Re-Alignment" of the path here by several feet to clear the corner of the building, but given the layout we may need to fully relocate the trail to the other side of the rock outcropping. This will be discussed at a future date once we see the condition after the excavation and foundation wall are poured. Various advantages to fully relocating the path included isolating the building more from the path, allowing room to install the security fence, and screening a possible location of the future emergency generator.
>
> 3. A higher retaining wall be needed at the east side door from Workshop, possibly up to 10' high (from the 4' height shown). We also discussed removing some of the rock off the top of this location to regrade and lower the height of the retaining wall.
>
> 4. Reviewed running of the foundation drains to the new storm water catch basins.
>
> 5. Reviewed possible locations for the future emergency generator, west side of the building was considered the best alternative.
>
> 6. Discussed enclosing the soffits and eaves to reduce insect & bird's nest problems, and maintenance issues. L&L to provide a possible detail for review.
>
